**C128.06.13 // Personal Log // Kaelen Starkman**  
**Exordium -> Amo, Metropolis**

I write this entry from a rented habitation capsule above Amo VIII. The station's artificial gravity hums beneath my feet, steady and predictable, unlike the chaos of the last few days.

My capsuleer career was supposed to begin differently.

Only hours after completing my final certification exercises within the Republic Military School training laboratories in the Exordium constellation, disaster struck. The station that had served as my introduction to capsuleer life was destroyed during a violent and unexplained attack. Rumors spread through local comms faster than verified reports. Some spoke of rogue capsuleers. Others whispered of pirate infiltrators or political sabotage. No official statement has yet identified those responsible.

What I remember most is the silence.

One moment the station was alive with instructors, technicians, and newly graduated capsuleers discussing their futures. The next, emergency alarms drowned out every conversation. By the time evacuation protocols were activated, the structure was already dying.

I survived.

Many did not.

I emerged from the wreckage with little more than my clone, a basic corvette, and a handful of ISK from the Republic's capsuleer support initiative.

For the first time in my life, I was truly alone.

As a Starkmanir, isolation is a familiar story. My tribe spent centuries scattered among the stars, our identity nearly erased by Amarr conquest. Yet standing among the refugees and survivors drifting through the aftermath, I understood that history in a way I never had before. We were all displaced. We were all searching for something to hold onto.

With no reason to remain in the devastated constellation, I boarded a Shipcaster transport network bound for the Metropolis Region. The transition was disorienting. One instant I was staring at the debris field that had been my home. The next, I emerged light-years away in Amo.

I had arrived physically.

Mentally, I was still adrift.

The stations of Amo felt enormous compared to the training facilities I had known. Pilots moved with confidence and purpose. Haulers negotiated contracts. Mission runners discussed combat fits. Industrialists calculated production chains worth more ISK than I had ever possessed.

I felt like an outsider.

For several days I wandered between hangars, markets, and training centers trying to determine what kind of capsuleer I would become. The dreams of reclaiming Starkmanir heritage and participating in the Restoration Campaign seemed impossibly distant. How could I help restore a lost tribe when I could barely afford ammunition?

Eventually I found my answer in the Republic's oldest lesson:

Start with what you have.

My first objective became simple.

Earn enough ISK to craft my own weapon systems.

The Republic campaign rewards offered a modest financial foundation. I invested every credit into basic projectile weapon manufacturing materials. It was not glamorous work. The industrial facilities smelled of coolant and hot metal. I spent hours learning blueprint interfaces, mineral requirements, and assembly procedures.

When the first autocannon emerged from the production line, scarred and imperfect, I felt more pride than I had during graduation.

I had built something with my own hands.

Tomorrow I intend to mount it on my vessel.

The local security offices have posted contracts against pirate elements operating within high-security systems. The targets are weak, little more than criminals preying upon miners and civilian traffic, but they will serve as suitable training.

I have also begun intensive instruction in gunnery theory. Every available simulation has been loaded into my neural queue:

- Basic Gunnery
- Small Projectile Turrets
- Motion Prediction
- Rapid Firing
- Sharpshooter
- Surgical Strike

The instructors claim mastery requires years.

I am prepared to begin with a single day.

Perhaps one day I will fly a Rifter worthy of the Starkmanir name. Perhaps one day I will stand in Arzad or Ezzara and contribute to the restoration of my people's sovereignty. Perhaps I will recover lost archives and forgotten knowledge hidden among the stars.

For now, however, my ambitions are smaller.

Survive.

Learn.

Fight.

The stars have taken everything familiar from me.

Now they will teach me who I am.

— Kaelen Starkman  
Capsuleer Candidate, Starkmanir  
Amo VIII, Metropolis Region  
End Log
