**Capsuleer Log — YC128.06.14**  
**Pilot: Kaelen Starkman**  
**Location: Amo, Metropolis**  
**Status: Venture Construction Underway**

The past few days have been a lesson in patience, persistence, and survival.

After completing several combat sorties against Angel Cartel forces operating in nearby systems, I managed to accumulate enough ISK through bounty hunting, AIR Opportunities, and rewards from the Minmatar campaign. The funds were modest, but sufficient for the next step in my journey: acquiring the blueprint needed to build a Venture-class mining frigate.

The nearest affordable blueprint was available through agents and markets associated with the Outer Ring Excavations corporation. Reaching it, however, proved far more dangerous than I anticipated.

The route took me through null-security space, where I quickly learned the harsh reality of capsuleer life. Warp disruption bubbles littered several stargates along my path. Despite my best efforts, I was caught repeatedly. Three times my ship was destroyed. Three times I awoke in a fresh clone vat, frustrated but determined.

Fortunately, I had prepared for the possibility.

Rather than transporting the original blueprint, I adopted a safer strategy. I produced a blueprint copy and carried only that across hostile space. The original remained secure. It was a small decision, but one that likely saved me from losing weeks of progress. Eventually, after numerous evasive maneuvers and a healthy amount of luck, I successfully transported the copy back to Amo without further incident.

With the blueprint secured, a new challenge emerged.

Building a Venture from scratch is no small undertaking for a newly licensed capsuleer.

Tritanium proved relatively easy to obtain. My humble corvette was more than capable of stripping local asteroid belts of Veldspar and Scordite. Within a few hours, I had stockpiled enough Tritanium for the hull components.

Mexallon, however, became my nemesis.

Plagioclase yielded only small quantities, and local belts were insufficient for my needs. After studying regional ore reports, I discovered a low-security system containing a Gneiss anomaly. The ore held exactly what I needed, but entering low-sec carried obvious risks.

I proceeded cautiously.

Before activating my mining lasers, I established multiple tactical bookmarks and emergency warp-out points around the anomaly. Every few minutes I checked directional scans and monitored local communications. The operation was progressing well until another capsuleer entered system.

The pilot, known as flyingfear94, was flying an Amarrian Crusader interceptor.

I barely had enough time to react.

The interceptor descended upon my position with terrifying speed. My corvette stood no chance. Within moments, the ship was reduced to expanding debris.

Fortunately, my preparations paid off. The moment the hull failed, I initiated an emergency warp and escaped with my pod intact. Losing the corvette was unfortunate, but far preferable to losing implants—or my hard-earned ore.

After several more cautious mining runs, I finally accumulated enough Mexallon to complete the material requirements.

The ore has now been refined.

The components have been manufactured.

The Venture assembly job is running at the station in Amo.

If all goes according to schedule, my first self-built ship will be completed tomorrow.

It is not an expensive vessel. Most veteran capsuleers would hardly notice it docked among their fleets. Yet for me, it represents something far greater than its market value.

Every unit of Tritanium was mined by my own hand.

Every unit of Mexallon was earned through risk and perseverance.

Every loss along the way taught a lesson.

Tomorrow I will undock not in a gifted corvette, but in a ship built entirely from my own labor.

Tomorrow is another day.

— Kaelen Starkman  
Vanguard of the Restoration  
Republic Capsuleer  
Amo Station, Metropolis
