The Rifter is an ugly prayer said at full burn.

I have flown cleaner hulls. I have sat inside ships with better capacitors, broader teeth, prettier diagnostics. None of them have ever told the truth as plainly as this old frame. It rattles when it is angry. It complains before the armor alarms do. It does not pretend survival is the same thing as control.

I named this one *Ash-Hymn* after the first recovery run into Ezzara. The bay smelled like scorched polymer for three days. The crew chief told me the venting would clear. It did not. Some smells become part of a ship because the ship has earned them.

There are scratches beside the pod cradle where my hand catches during emergency exit. There is a patch of darker plating near the starboard feed where a beam nearly opened us from nose to spine. There is a cargo clamp that still jams when I load relic containers too quickly.

I keep meaning to fix it.

I do not.

The Rifter remembers because I let it. Every mark is an argument against erasure. Every repair that leaves a scar is a better archive than a perfect replacement.

The hull is fast enough to run, sharp enough to bite, and cheap enough that no one in procurement asks why I keep requesting another. They think it is sentiment. They are not wrong.

But sentiment is not weakness. Sentiment is logistics for the soul.
