The first patrol through Arzad gave me silence, and silence in low security space is a liar with clean hands.

I took the Rifter out past the station lights and let the overview settle into its familiar amber wounds: wrecks, cans, customs offices, signatures with no manners. Every gate in this system feels like a question someone already bled over. You do not enter Arzad so much as submit yourself to its memory.

The ship handled like it remembered the route better than I did. Two short burns. One wide spiral around a dead pocket. Combat probes on scan for seventeen seconds, then gone. I logged the vector anyway.

No relic recovery today. No data core. No clean fragment with a name I could return to anyone. Only the old pressure behind the ribs, the knowledge that sovereignty has to be touched to be real. A claim in a database is a ghost. A patrol is a hand on the doorframe.

At 22:14, I marked a wreck near the third belt. Civilian hull, stripped beyond registry. Someone had carved three lines into an exposed plate. Not Amarr. Not Republic issue. Maybe nothing. Maybe a child marking height on a wall that no longer exists.

I brought the plate back.

Some days restoration is not glorious. Some days it is a small piece of metal in the cargo hold and the refusal to let silence finish its work.
