I keep hearing the word sovereignty used like a weapon someone is proud to show off.

Hold the grid. Claim the system. Own the route. Control the structure. Plant the flag. Count the timers. Win.

Capsuleers love verbs that fit inside a combat report.

The Starkmanir Restoration needs different verbs. Recover. Verify. Return. Teach. Mourn. Repair. Refuse.

Conquest asks who can take a place. Restoration asks who has been kept from returning to it. The answer is rarely clean enough for propaganda. It is names split across registries, songs mistranslated by priests, children taught that survival required forgetting which words belonged to their grandparents.

I patrol because patrols keep predators honest. I fight because sometimes the only way to protect an archive is to put autocannons between it and someone else's appetite. But the fight is not the point. If the last thing we build here is another throne with better slogans, then we have only changed the metal of the cage.

Arzad does not need me to conquer it.

It needs me to listen long enough to learn what was already here.

It needs us to stop mistaking possession for belonging.
